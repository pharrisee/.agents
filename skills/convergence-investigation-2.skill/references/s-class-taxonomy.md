## S-Class Definitions (Selected)

### Category: Institutional Capture & Control

**S-3A — Fiduciary Bridge**
- **Definition:** Personal fiduciary instrument (POA, trustee, executor) serves as structural bridge between domains
- **Mechanism:** Legal authority granted to individual enables operational control across organizational boundaries
- **Indicators:** POA documents, trustee appointments, executor designations
- **Duration:** Often multi-decade persistence
- **Example:** POA held by a trusted financial advisor over a principal's personal and philanthropic affairs, persisting through a role transition from advisor to foundation trustee and subsequent estate administration

**S-3B — Operational Capture**
- **Definition:** Philanthropic/institutional function gradually redirects toward operational/criminal purposes
- **Mechanism:** Legitimate institution co-opted for secondary function
- **Indicators:** Mission drift, funding allocation shifts, staff turnover
- **Example:** [To be documented in future investigations]

### Category: Multi-Generational Patterns

**S-42A — Direct Lineage**
- **Definition:** Parent-child transfer of institutional access or operational capability
- **Mechanism:** Explicit inheritance of roles, contacts, methods
- **Example:** [To be documented]

**S-42B — Pre-existing Condition (Temporal Lineage)**
- **Definition:** Institutional access predates subject involvement; subject leverages pre-existing opening
- **Mechanism:** Family/associate pre-positioning creates later exploitable structure
- **Indicators:** Donations/board seats from family members preceding subject entry
- **Duration:** Can span decades (e.g., a parent donates to and joins the board of an institution, and a child later introduces a third-party subject into that same institution years or decades afterwards)
- **Example:** Parent as donor and board member from Year 0; sibling on the same board from Year 0; subject introduced via the sibling ~Year 5–10

### Category: Deferred/Concealed Transactions

**S-17A — Immediate Concealment**
- **Definition:** Transaction executed with contemporaneous omission from records
- **Mechanism:** Deliberate non-disclosure at time of execution
- **Example:** [To be documented]

**S-17B — Delayed Disclosure**
- **Definition:** Transaction disclosed but with significant temporal lag
- **Mechanism:** Active operation during "dark period" before eventual revelation
- **Example:** [To be documented]

**S-17C — Deferred Formalization**
- **Definition:** Agreement/transaction executed but documentation formalized years later
- **Mechanism:** 13+ year gaps between agreement and title/recording
- **Indicators:** Promissory notes with delayed registration, handshake deals formalized post-facto
- **Example:** Real-estate transfer in Year 0 funded by a promissory note and personal guaranty, with title transfer not formally recorded until Year 13

### Category: Financial Structures

**S-7A — Circular Funding**
- **Definition:** Funds flow through multiple entities ultimately returning to origin
- **Mechanism:** Series of transactions obscures ultimate source/beneficiary
- **Example:** [To be documented]

**S-7B — Beneficial Owner Concealment**
- **Definition:** Legal ownership separated from beneficial ownership via nominee structures
- **Mechanism:** Trusts, shell companies, bearer instruments
- **Example:** USVI LLCs → Bermuda SIV → Delaware trusts chain

### Category: Personnel Patterns

**S-12A — Revolving Door**
- **Definition:** Personnel rotate between regulatory/corporate and regulated/target positions
- **Mechanism:** Institutional knowledge transfer; potential capture
- **Example:** [To be documented]

**S-12B — Dual Hat**
- **Definition:** Individual simultaneously holds roles in theoretically separate domains
- **Mechanism:** Single person bridges multiple institutional contexts
- **Examples:** An attorney who simultaneously serves as counsel to a client and as negotiator for that client's counterparty foundation; a board member of an institution who is also an operational associate of a subject the board does not formally transact with

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Apply S-Classes

**Primary Use:** Cross-investigation pattern recognition. When you identify an S-3A in Investigation A, search for S-3A patterns in Investigation B.

**Secondary Use:** Structural parallel documentation. When comparison tables include parallels meeting the methodology bar, optional S-class tagging adds searchable vocabulary.

### S-Class Assignment Process

1. **Identify candidate pattern** during structural parallels sub-method
2. **Compare to S-class definitions** above
3. **If match:** Tag parallel with S-class in comparison table
4. **Document instance:** Add specific example to this file (expand taxonomy corpus)
5. **Cross-reference:** Note if similar S-class appeared in other investigations

### Limits

**NOT required:** S-class tagging is optional. The structural parallels sub-method functions without it.

**NOT exhaustive:** This is a selected subset of WFGY 3.0's 131 S-classes, chosen for relevance to OSINT investigation.

**Experimental:** As more investigations use S-classes, definitions may need refinement. Version this document.
