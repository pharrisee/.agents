# GitHub Wiki Fact Checker Skill

Use this skill to fact-check content in a GitHub wiki (gh-wiki) against original sources, verify claims, identify unsupported assertions, and maintain accuracy in the knowledge base.

## When to Use This Skill

- Verifying factual claims in wiki pages
- Checking source citations for accuracy and completeness
- Detecting contradictions between different wiki pages
- Maintaining provenance and traceability of information
- Validating assertions against authoritative sources

## When NOT to Use This Skill

- **Simple fact checks**: Single, easily verifiable facts (dates, names, numbers) — answer directly
- **Personal opinions**: Subjective assessments or editorial content — not suitable for fact-checking
- **Out-of-scope content**: Non-gh-wiki content or sources outside the wiki scope
- **Legal/compliance matters**: Content requiring legal review or regulatory compliance verification
- **Automated verification**: Claims requiring human judgment or nuanced interpretation
- **Unverifiable assertions**: Claims without possible source verification (hearsay, speculation)

## Core Purpose

This skill provides systematic methods to:
1. **Verify claims** against original sources
2. **Identify unsupported assertions** in wiki pages
3. **Check source citations** for accuracy and completeness
4. **Detect contradictions** between different wiki pages
5. **Maintain provenance** and traceability of information

## Implementation Notes

Always prioritize primary sources over secondary references. Flag unverifiable claims clearly and distinguish between verified facts and best-effort verification.
